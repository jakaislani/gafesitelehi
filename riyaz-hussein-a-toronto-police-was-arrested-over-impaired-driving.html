Was He arrested for drunk driving? Investigating a Jail Sentence

A high-ranking Toronto police officer, the force’s disciplinary tribunal director, was arrested for drunk driving. Following a collision with a delivery truck, Supt. Riyaz Hussein was arrested by Ontario Provincial Police on Thursday evening. Hussein, 54, of Pickering, was charged with careless driving and open liquor after a two-car accident just after 7 p.m., according to the OPP. According to a tweet from the OPP’s Highway Safety Division, the driver rear-ended a delivery car and received minor injuries.

Hussein is said to have been fired from the Toronto Police Department. He could not be contacted immediately for comment. Hussein is currently the officer in chief of the force’s disciplinary hearings office, a quasi-judicial tribunal that oversees officer misconduct, including criminal violations like driving while drunk.
